About the Record

We couldn’t be more excited about this effort. It kicks off with the blistering single and title track, "Burning Sound." From there, "Only" effortlessly traverses time signatures, layered with sax and trombone, while "Lightmare" gets massive with rich cello and a bombastic rhythm section. We sprinkle interstitial pieces to bring you along on this adventure. "Almost Home" rounds things out with textural organ, guiding you through oddly jaunty verses and anthemic choruses begging you to “Save the soul of the song!”

It’s a dynamic journey that we can't wait to get out into the world.

The Packaging: Hand-Crafted & Collectible - The music makes a statement and the physical presentation will too.

The Record: 10” red vinyl.

The Cover: Designed and letterpressed by Jeff Mueller (Dexterity Letterpress). Jeff has created iconic letterpress work for bands like Shellac, Yo La Tengo, Palace, Bardo Pond, Savak, and others; alongside his own legendary projects (Rodan, June of 44, Shipping News).

The Detail: Each heavy-stock 10" jacket will be individually letterpressed with Jeff's impressive design and will also feature our logo literally burned into the back cover with an actual custom-made Discount Fireworks branding iron.

The Sound: The record is mixed by Travis Harrison, best known for his long time collaboration with indie-rock legends, Guided By Voices (producer, engineer, mixer, live FOH and collaborator). Additionally he has worked with Built to Spill, Savak, The Boredoms, Fred Armisen, OK Go, The Men, Dope Body, and many more.

The Supporting Cast: We invited our friends to the party, making this album greater than the sum of its parts: Karyn Watts (Civic Orchestra of New Haven) makes "Lightmare" bigger and more menacing with her cello. Ska legend Vinny Nobile (Bim Skala Bim, Pilfers, Cenzo) injects atmosphere and hype into "Only" with his trombone blasts and bleats. Richard Brown (Mark Mulcahy/Miracle Legion, The Sawtelles, Happy Ending) adds sax and guitar, steering "Only" and "Pinnacles" to deeper places. Scott Amore (Mark Mulcahy/Miracle Legion, The Butterflies of Love, Famous Problems) layers texture and tension onto "Almost Home" with his Leslie organ. And Stella Vlad (Least Best Beast, Speakeasy Tattoo) closes the record with witchy wisdom on the finale "Burning Out."

This won't just be a record; it will be a beautifully crafted, limited-edition art piece.
